Why it persists

Acne in adults is a chronic condition, not a phase.

  • The products you're using aren't clinical-grade.
    Consumer skincare caps active concentrations well below therapeutic thresholds. A 2% glycolic cleanser can't do what a 10% AHA protocol does. The formulations are different. The results are different.
  • The stress connection is being ignored.
    Cortisol directly increases sebum production and weakens the skin barrier. Most acne products don't acknowledge this mechanism at all. They treat the oil without treating the signal that produces it.
  • Late-onset acne is increasing.
    25% of adults over 25 now present with persistent or new-onset acne. It disproportionately affects women. It correlates with modern stress patterns. And it requires a fundamentally different treatment approach.
  • Without intervention, the cycle compounds.
    Stress elevates cortisol. Cortisol increases sebum. Sebum causes breakouts. Breakouts cause anxiety. Anxiety elevates cortisol. This is a documented clinical loop. It doesn't resolve on its own.
